YOU MUST BE NEW. HEY THERE!

My name is Humphrey Obuobi. I generally like making things.

I'm a sophomore at Harvard studying bioengineering and computer science, largely because I'm passionate about a lot of different things and simply couldn't choose one thing.

If there's one thing I'm certain about, it's the importance of good design, whether you're talking about the next big advance in wearable technology or making a menu for a diner.

I MAKE THINGS FOR IMPACT.
BUD
A desktop companion resembling a flower that blooms and wilts according to the weather and vital signs of the user. Created at Hack@Brown.
SHINDO
A communication wearable that translates text into vibrations for communication with deaf-blind individuals. Created at Assistive Tech Makeathon with Brown and RISD STEAM.
MONITOR STAND
A lightweight stand for a portable monitor that uses the stand's own geometry and the weight of the monitor itself to balance and adjust the angle.
